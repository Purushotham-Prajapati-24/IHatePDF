# 1. Customer Journey Map (CJM): The "Earned Donation" Flow

In a donation model, you cannot lock features. You must deliver overwhelming value first, and then politely ask for support at the exact moment of highest user satisfaction.

| Stage | User Goal | Actions | Emotions | Pain Points | System / UX Opportunities |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1. Discovery | Find a safe, free PDF tool. | Searches "merge pdf completely free private". | Skeptical. Expects hidden paywalls or watermarks. | "Free" usually means data harvesting. | Homepage hero explicitly states: "100% Free. Runs locally. Your files never leave your computer." |
| 2. Engagement | Merge 5 sensitive tax documents. | Drags files into the drop zone. | Surprised. | Expecting a slow upload bar. | Show a "Processing Locally..." badge. The UI reacts instantly because there is no network request. |
| 3. Activation | Get the final file. | Clicks "Merge". File saves instantly to their local downloads folder. | Delighted. The "Aha!" moment of zero latency. | None. | Trigger confetti or a smooth success animation. Show exactly how much time/bandwidth they saved. |
| 4. The "Ask" (Donation) | Continue with their day. | Sees the success screen. | Grateful for the frictionless experience. | Pop-ups or aggressive begging. | Show a subtle, highly transparent card: "We saved you X minutes and kept your data 100% private. Keep us ad-free by buying the developer a coffee." |
| 5. Retention & Advocacy | Remember the tool for next time. | Bookmarks the site; shares the link with colleagues. | Empowered. Trusts the platform completely. | Forgetting |  |
